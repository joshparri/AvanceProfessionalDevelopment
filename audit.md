The two Avance apps you’ve been building have come a long way.  In many places they already embody the workflow assumptions and backlog items outlined in your internal documents and chats.  Below is a summary of how they align with your day‑to‑day responsibilities, what still feels missing, and a SWOT analysis based on the websites and backlog spreadsheet.

### How the apps support your role and responsibilities

* **Shift planning and time tracking.** Both apps let you define Monday/Wednesday shifts, start/stop them and see upcoming shifts and hours logged.  This aligns with your need to juggle Dubbo school work and Avance MSP shifts, and to document billable hours.
* **Quick capture and work‑log evidence.** Each app has a “Quick Capture” form for work logs, tasks and follow‑ups.  You can tag work (support, admin, learning), note client names, add follow‑ups, and keep logs safe from client PII.  This matches the backlog’s emphasis on capturing work details (“What happened, what I checked, what I found, action, outcome”) and supports your note‑taking responsibilities.
* **MSP skills and scenario training.** The Professional‑Development app includes an “MSP Skills Matrix” and “Scenario Trainer,” where you can practice ticket triage, diagnostic questions and safe checks.  These directly help you grow in areas like first‑pass troubleshooting, safe escalation and note‑writing, which are part of your role (as per the workflow assumptions).  The quiz and learning cockpit gamify progress and suggest next training moves.
* **Ticket‑notes templates and evidence pack.** There’s a ticket‑note trainer with a seven‑part structure (issue, impact, checks, action, result, next step, escalation) and examples of poor/okay/excellent notes.  The Evidence Pack summarises skills practised, activities done and minutes logged, producing a manager‑safe snapshot.  This speaks to the backlog’s need for clear ticket notes and manager‑ready evidence.
* **Healthy work habits.** The new “Health & Outdoors” module encourages hydration, daylight breaks, posture resets, micro‑movement, 20‑20‑20 eye breaks and a shutdown ritual.  It offers quick actions (drink water, add outdoor minutes, eye break, posture reset) and a weekly review with prompts and research‑backed ideas.  This addresses your desire to avoid over‑focus and stay grounded during MSP shifts.

### What still feels missing

* **Integrated ticket intake and escalation workflow.** The apps don’t yet pull tickets from Halo or integrate with 3CX phone logs.  Your blueprint notes a “ticket intake model” where tickets come via phone, email or monitoring alerts, with statuses like New, In Progress and Awaiting Invoice.  The current apps rely on manual work‑logs rather than surfacing live ticket queues or reminding you to change statuses, assign urgency or mark “Awaiting Invoice.”
* **Onsite and quote workflows.** The backlog mentions onsite workflow (checking keys, gear, confirming contact, sending follow‑up) and quote workflow (gather requirements, device counts, photos, scope notes, estimate labour, update Halo ticket, then pass to Andy/Ned).  The apps don’t yet provide guided checklists for these processes.  Integrating simple checklists or playbooks for onsite jobs and quoting would close a gap.
* **Tool‑selector or escalation guidance.** The workflow assumptions call for a “source‑of‑truth model” (live system → Halo tickets → client documentation → Keeper → what the client says → ask James/Andy) and common issue playbook categories (email not working, new user setup, Wi‑Fi down, etc.).  While the PD app has skill cards, it lacks a quick “issue playbook” or first‑check logic tree that guides you through which tool or portal to open first.
* **Real‑time collaboration and chat context.** The backlog spreadsheet includes tasks like Ironscales integration, laptop setups, performance troubleshooting and migrations.  Those tasks often involve back‑and‑forth in Google Chat or email.  The apps don’t surface conversation context or link directly to the relevant Google Chat threads; you still need to switch tools to recall details or confirm instructions.
* **Billing and quoting.** The PD app’s dashboard shows an “invoice cycle” card but doesn’t help with preparing invoices or quoting steps.  The backlog emphasises correct billing (e.g., ensuring non‑MSA clients are invoiced, tracking hourly rates), and the workflow assumptions include Ned’s role in quotes.  A simple quoting module or invoice checklist could streamline this.

### Overall impression

Both Avance apps provide a solid foundation for your MSP role: they structure shifts, logs and learning; encourage healthy work habits; and respect client privacy.  However, they still lack deep integration with your ticketing and quoting systems and don’t yet provide the on‑site/quote playbooks and first‑check decision trees outlined in your workflow assumptions.  Addressing these gaps—and ensuring the apps continue to evolve with your role—will make them even more valuable companions in your ICT and library support work.
