## CLIENT PROFILE & MARKET CHARACTERISTICS

### Primary Client Types

**Accounting Firms**
- Pain points: Legacy systems, user access management, backup/compliance
- Common issues: Password resets, file share permissions, email problems
- Budget-conscious but compliance-aware
- Typically 5–20 employees

**Law Firms**
- Pain points: Data security, client confidentiality, email archiving
- Common issues: Similar to accounting, plus document management
- Relatively sophisticated about IT needs
- Often have conflicts of interest across clients

**Trades/Construction**
- Pain points: Field worker connectivity, invoicing/billing systems
- Common issues: Mobile access, printer connectivity, Windows-based job costing software
- May have aging systems
- Typically 5–50 employees

**Schools/Educational**
- Pain points: Student privacy, NDIS compliance (if relevant), classroom tech
- Common issues: WiFi capacity, teacher device management, parent communication systems
- Often grant-funded (tight budgets)
- Regulatory compliance important

**General SMB Pattern:**
- Too small to employ full-time IT (1–2 person IT shops max)
- Too large to ignore IT (systems are mission-critical)
- Price-sensitive but value-conscious
- Relationships matter (often know the MSP personally)

### Dubbo Market Characteristics

**Population:** ~35,000 (city), ~52,000 (regional area)

**Economy:**
- Agriculture (sheep, cattle, dairy—declining)
- Government services (regional hub)
- Healthcare (Western Cancer Centre draws patients)
- Hospitality & tourism
- Services (accounting, law, trades)

**IT Market Dynamics:**
- Small player pool (limited competition from major MSPs)
- Strong community/relationship focus
- Price-conscious (regional budgets tend to be tighter)
- Less tech-forward than Sydney/Melbourne
- Higher need for remote support (traveling field workers)

**Staffing Challenges:**
- Young people leave for city opportunities
- Limited local IT talent pool
- Brain drain to Sydney/Brisbane/Melbourne
- Reliance on training from TAFE (which 2019 article noted was "hopeless" for practical IT skills)
